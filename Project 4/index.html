The "Peter Pan" of the Animal Kingdom

The Axolotl is a species of salamander native only to the lake complex of Xochimilco near Mexico City. Unlike other amphibians that undergo metamorphosis to live on land, the axolotl exhibits neoteny, meaning it retains its juvenile traits—like feathery external gills and a finned tail—throughout its entire adult life.

Did You Know?

The Axolotl can regenerate entire limbs, spinal cord, heart tissue, and even parts of its brain.

Interesting Facts

  • Axolotls are critically endangered in the wild due to habitat loss and pollution.
  • They can grow up to 30 cm (12 inches) in length.
  • Axolotls are often used in scientific research due to their regenerative abilities.
